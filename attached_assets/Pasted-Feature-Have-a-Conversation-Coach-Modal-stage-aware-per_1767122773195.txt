Feature: “Have a Conversation” Coach Modal (stage-aware + persona-aware)

We need a “Have a Conversation” coaching action that appears when the system detects the account is stalled despite completing the normal steps (quotes/samples sent, follow-ups attempted, category trust not progressing, no purchase).

1) Trigger logic (when to show)

Show a “📞 Have a Conversation” CTA as the Next Best Move when ALL are true:

Account has at least one relevant category at Introduced or Evaluated

There is an Open or Expired quote older than X days (configurable, default 7–14)

OR a Press Test Kit/Sample is Sent and outcome is missing beyond X days (default 7–10)

No new order for this category in last X days (default 30)

The usual nudges have already fired (quote follow-up, sample follow-up)
=> This indicates a human uncertainty blocker.

Clicking CTA opens a modal (or right-side panel) with calling scripts.

2) Modal behavior (no typing required)

Modal includes:

A header showing: Account name, Account State (Prospect / Expansion), and Persona (Distributor / Dealer / End Customer)

A script selector with tabs:

Prospect scripts

Expansion scripts

Distributor scripts

End Customer scripts
(Auto-select the correct tab based on account state + machine type selection of Distributor/Dealer)

Each script is shown as short “talk tracks” (1–2 sentences) with “Copy” button.

At bottom, a quick outcome capture (buttons only):

✅ Next step agreed

🟡 Still undecided

❌ Not moving forward
Optional quick reason buttons (no free text):

Price / Value unclear / Timing / Compatibility / Supplier locked / Internal approval / Not a fit

Log this as an event so owner can see why deals stall.

3) Script library (content required)

A) Prospect stage scripts (first purchase not yet happened)
Goal: uncover the real blocker politely and quickly.

Permission opener:

“Hey [Name] — quick check-in. Is this still something you’re considering, or should I step back for now?”

Missing piece question:

“I feel like we’ve covered the basics. What am I missing that’s keeping this from moving?”

Compare vs inertia:

“Are you comparing us against what you already use, or deciding whether to change at all?”

Value vs budget:

“When price comes up, is it more about budget, or about whether the value makes sense?”

Next-step clarity:

“If we were to move forward, what would you need to feel comfortable doing that?”

B) Expansion stage scripts (customer already buys something; we want more categories)
Goal: expand without sounding pushy; focus on standardization + risk reduction.

Low-pressure expansion:

“You’re already using [current category]. Would it be useful if I showed you the closest alternatives we have for [adjacent category]?”

Standardization framing:

“Most shops running [machine type] usually standardize on 2–3 materials. Want me to suggest the simplest set?”

Blocker identification:

“What’s the main reason you haven’t tried [next category] yet — timing, fit, or just no need?”

Trial ask:

“Would you be open to a small test pack so you can compare it on your own press?”

Relationship move (high-frequency buyer):

“Since you’re ordering regularly, would a stocking plan or bundle pricing make your life easier?”

C) Distributor scripts (they care about margin, demand, and support)
Goal: understand their channel motion and make it easy to list/stock.

Fit + demand:

“What would you need to see to feel confident there’s demand for this in your channel?”

Margin clarity:

“Is the hesitation mostly margin structure, or sell-through confidence?”

Enablement:

“If I gave you a simple sell-sheet + sample pack for your reps, would that make it easier to move?”

Trial stock:

“Would you consider a small starter stocking order so you can test sell-through?”

Disqualify politely:

“If this isn’t aligned with your category focus right now, should we revisit later or drop it?”

D) End-customer scripts (print shop / direct buyer)
Goal: reduce perceived risk (compatibility, quality, switching cost).

Risk reduction:

“What’s the biggest risk you’re trying to avoid — compatibility, customer acceptance, or downtime?”

Supplier lock-in:

“Are you happy with your current supplier, or just trying to avoid the hassle of switching?”

Proof request:

“If we ran a quick test and you liked the results, would you be ready to place an order?”

Timing:

“Is this a ‘not now’ problem, or a ‘not sure’ problem?”

4) Let-go rules (clean disqualification)

If outcome is ❌ Not moving forward:

mark current quote as “Closed-Lost”

set a “Revisit date” suggestion (30/60/90 days) but do NOT create tasks

store reason code

Next Best Move should stop nagging and instead show:
“Paused — revisit in X days” (only visible to rep)

If outcome is 🟡 Still undecided:

Next Best Move should propose 1 action:

“Log objection” OR “Send sample” OR “Schedule follow-up”
(based on reason selected)

Acceptance criteria

Reps can open scripts and run the call without typing.

Scripts adapt to Prospect vs Expansion and Distributor vs End Customer.

Outcome is captured in 1 click and stored.

System uses outcomes to stop endlessly repeating the same nudges.

Extra guidance (so Replit doesn’t botch it)

Keep scripts short; reps should not sound robotic.

This modal must feel like a coach, not compliance.

Do NOT add a general “task list” screen for this feature.

My recommended “Let go” policy (for you, not the UI)

If you want a clean rule your team can live with:

Prospect: If 2 full cycles of follow-up (quote + sample) produce no response, mark as “Paused” and set a 60-day revisit.

Expansion: If they buy consistently but reject expansion twice, pause that category and try a different adjacent category later.

Distributor: If they won’t commit to a starter stock plan after margin + enablement is provided, pause and revisit quarterly.

This keeps your pipeline honest and your reps sane.
